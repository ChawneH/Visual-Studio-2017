History & Preamble

In 1995, the Collegiate 100 was founded on the campus of Prairie View A&M University by Frederick V. Roberts. The Jackson State University Chapter was charted on April 12, 2018 with the charge to be a responsible, productive, and cooperative segment of the local sponsoring 100 Black Men of America, Inc. Chapter and to be an active organization for the JSU community

The Collegiate 100 of JSU adds value to its community by serving as a corps of service volunteers to help carry out the mission of the 100 Black Men of America, Inc.

Mentoring

The primary purpose of the Collegiate 100® program is to provide an avenue for 100 Black Men chapters to continue their one-on-one and group mentoring to students as they matriculate from high school to college; thereby supporting the Mentoring The 100 Way® Across a Lifetime™ initiatives.

Education

The Collegiate 100® program has an important role to play on the college campus. The effective execution of that role requires that the Collegiate 100® program be a responsible, productive, and cooperative segment of the local Sponsoring 100 Black Men Chapter and an active student organization on the college campus and in the local community.

Empowerment

The Collegiate 100® adds value by serving as a core of service volunteers to help carry out the mission of the 100 Black Men of America, Inc. and support the activities of the Sponsoring Chapter.
